Biography

A concise academic profile centered on reinforcement learning and visual decision-making.

I am a Ph.D. student in Artificial Intelligence at the School of Software, Shandong University, affiliated with the Artificial Intelligence Research Center and Time Lab. My research focuses on deep reinforcement learning, including visual reinforcement learning, inverse reinforcement learning, and multi-task reinforcement learning.

Before beginning my doctoral study, I studied at the School of Software, Shandong University, and was recommended for direct admission to the Ph.D. program. I look forward to continuing my research journey at Shandong University.

Research Interests

My current interests connect perception, policy learning, and generalization in reinforcement learning.

01

Deep Reinforcement Learning

Learning robust and generalizable decision-making policies through reinforcement learning.

02

Visual Reinforcement Learning

Training agents from visual observations and improving policy generalization under visual variations.

03

Inverse Reinforcement Learning

Studying how reward signals and behavior objectives can be inferred from demonstrations or behavior.

04

Multi-task Reinforcement Learning

Exploring transferable behavior and shared representations across related reinforcement learning tasks.
